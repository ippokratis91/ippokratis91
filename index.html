2 Way Coaxial Speaker

I manufactured a 2-way speaker system, with two drives in coaxial arrangement and Bass Reflex port. For the implementation of this speaker, i used the Focal's drive RCX-130, which has a frequency response from 65 Hz to 21 KHz. The Woofer Driver has a cone diameter: 130 mm and Tweeter Driver includes an inverted dome. The system for input voltage: 2.83 V, produces a Sensitivity: 90 dB (S.P.L) at 1m. The speaker has a Rated Power 50 W(RMS)and Impendeance 4 Ohm. The Volume of the cabinet has the Dimentions: 167x200x280 mm. Finally, the cabinet is made of MDF Panels 12 mm thickness with wood finishing and the backview contains the Absorb material and the Connections.

Design PA Address for Tunnels

This thesis summarize a research for Public Address Systems which have applications in road tunnels. More specific, the research concerns the choice of a suitable audio system, which will assure that the most listeners will receive optimal sound, for Maliakos – Klidi road tunnel. Thus in a case of emergency, to perceive correctly the sound and evacuate safely the road tunnel. Two proposals system configurations are for tunnel Maliakos – Klidi: The first is about successive horns placed at every 50 m at same direction. The second is about paired horns at opposite direction placed at every 100 m. For each configuration variants of cases have been studied, through paper calculations. For each configuration the best case was chosen, with a criterion that all listeners will receive smooth sound pressure level distribution. The next step was a simulation with the CATT Acoustic software, that were studied acoustic parameters related to speech quality, such as STI, U50, D50 etc. In order to verified which of the two cases produces the best sound for the most listener positions. The firsts chapters report general concepts about Public Address Systems for tunnels. In addition mention general concepts about CATT Acoustic software. For example the kinds of acoustic parameters which the simulation can calculate. Furthermore the thesis presents paper calculations and calculations through the software at each step.

Design and Manufacture Class D Integrated Amplifier

Design and Manufacture, Class D, Stereo, Integrated Amplifier, on the verge of university dissertation. The amplifier produces Power Output: 2x15 W(RMS), for speakers with 8 Ohm Impendance and 15 V Power Supply. The amplifier contains, 2 Digital Sound Inputs (SPDIF Interfaces), One Toslink/Optical input and One Coaxial input, with RCA Connector. The Amplifier consists of 3 sub-systems: The first contains an SPDIF IC, which receives the signal, from a digital source and one DAC which converts, the digital signal to analogue signal. The Second Stage is a pre-Amp, with Equalizer, for the Sound Signal Processing. There are three, potentiometers: BASS, MIDDLE, TREBLE responsible for Level correction, in low frequencies, middle frequency spectrum and higher frequency spectrum. In addition a potentiometer is responsible, for Master Level Volume. Finally the 3rd sub-system, is the power stage, which drives, the speakers. It contains the TI's IC TPA3130 which operates, as a Class D amplifier.
